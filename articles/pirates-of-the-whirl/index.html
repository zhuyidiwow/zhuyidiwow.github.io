Detailed Design Notes

1 - Brainstorm & Initial idea

Brainstorm of this game is fairly easy, or, almost doesn't exist. I had the idea of a sailing game. I love the feeling of sailing across the sea and conquering all kinds of dangers along the way. This idea itself is solid, "automatically themed" and can generate many interesting gameplay mechanics, so I felt confident with it and decided to go with it.

So, the initial idea is: make a game centered around sailing and dices.

And the I came up with the problem statement:

  1. Make a fun and engaging table top game for 16 - 25 years old people, which can be played in roughly 15 - 30 minutes play sessions. Potentially a good fit to be played during nerd parties
  2. Use dices as the center for all mechanics
  3. Give the player some space for strategic planning while keeping a sense of uncertainty (which is, a balance of skill and chance)

Then I came up with the first rule set.

2 - Initial Set of Rules

This is a sailing & trading & battling game happens on the sea. The first game includes: a map, cards, ship tokens, account books, and two dices.

Set up

In the beginning, each player choose his/her ship and starting harbor, and have the chance to buy some goods. Everyone's starting fund is 2,000. Everyone also gets an account book to mark down transactions.

Map

  1. Island: no one can sail on islands
  2. Whirl: if one stays in the whirl, in his/her rolling stage:
    1. If two dices are equal, lose half cargo, else:
    2. Draw one card
    3. Nanda & Flola: they are neutral cities, randomly sell goods.

Flow

  1. Everyone take turns to roll two dices:
    1. Choose one as the tailwind dice, another as dead wind dice. Movement point is the difference between them. If they equal to each other, the player cannot move, but can draw a card.
    2. Pirate ship: At the end of each round, two players each roll one dice. The first dice determines the number of steps the pirate ship can move, the second dice determines the direction: 1-North, and rotate clockwise (2-Northeast, etc).
      1. If pirate ship gets adjacent to a player ship, that player ship sink.
      2. A ship can trade with port when get adjacent to the port or stops in the port.

Cards

(Cards are not included in this document)

  1. If not specified, one can use action card at anytime
  2. Event cards are triggered when drawn
  3. Improv cards are used when drawn

3 - Iterations

3.1 - First iteration

Analysis:

Playtesters: Yidi himself testing in his head.

The pirate ship looks like a good idea, but it's hard to find a convenient and clean way to move it, and it's hard to decide an appropriate penalty. In the first place, I add the pirate ship to increase interactions between players, but this seems like not the best way.

Modifications

Remove the pirate ship, and add the following rule:

  1. If a player(A) sails onto or adjacent to another player (B)'s tile, battle:
    1. Each of them roll two dices:
      1. if A is higher: A get half of B's cargo
      2. Else: B is free to move 2 tiles

This rule will vastly increase the interaction between players, I think it's promising

Potential problem

  1. Now I'm not sure whether the board I'm using is big enough. And the dice roll rules seem unusual. Focus on them in the next playtest

3.2 - Second iteration

Erhan (ETC 1st year) and I played the game.

Analysis

Erhan was confused with most rules, even I explained them in our native language. So I think it's a good idea to make more clarifications for rules.

Modifications

So, this iteration focuses on clarification of rules.

  1. If a ship stops in the whirl or get dragged into the whirl, it's stuck. When stuck:
    1. The player need to roll a number that's larger than 9 to get out. The player can choose one of the tile adjacent to the whirl. And his/her turn ends.
    2. A ship can use partial movement points to stop in a port. When a ship stops in a port, the ship can trade with the port. After trading, the ship can use remaining movement points.
    3. After dice roll and movement, draw one card.

3.3 - Third iteration

Lotus (ETC 1st year), Xuejun (ETC 1st year), Erhan (ETC 1st year) and I played the game.

People love the unusual dice roll, and they enjoyed the game! And the potential problem listed in iteration 1 seems to be not a problem at all.

Analysis

They loved the rich interations between players, the mechanics of the whirld, the event and action cards, and the rich system. However, the numerical system of this version is totally messed up! For example, Xuejun got a "full sail" cards, which increases her movement speed by 100% during one turn, and she rolled a 6 and a 1, which gives her a total of 5 * 2 = 10 movement points! She sailed across the sea and got an easy victory.

Beside that, there is still many confusions about the rules.

One final thing, Erhan seems to not like the improv cards - he always discard them. This is not a good signal.

Modifications

More clarifications:

  1. Battle is a choice
  2. Can't battle ships that are in the whirld or port
  3. Each neutral port only sell one good at a time
  4. Inflation also affect new goods
  5. Two inflation cards means: x1.4. Not x 1.44

I also did some modifications to the cards. One major change is to the "full sail" cards, I changed the extra movement point from 100% to 50%. The reason why I have this card is to give the player the chance to move faster while still taking some risk. Because this card can only be played before dice roll, so its effectiveness still depend on the dice roll. If the player rolls a 2 + 1, the card only gives 1 extra movement points. If the player rolls a 6 + 1, the card gives 3 extra movement points.

3.4 - Fourth Iteration

Tommy (a CS major from GCS), Euna (ETC 1st year), SorryIForgotHisNameA (ETC 2nd year), and SorryIForgotHisNameB (a business major) played this game during the class.

Analysis

They thought the game was surely fun! But the numercial system is totally broken, and the improv cards can be either awkward or interesting, all depending on the people.

They gave many nice suggestions, such as making improv cards compulsory, limiting the cargo capacity. They also felt it's so hard to get out of the whirl, and the neutral ports are not really helpful.

Modifications
  1. Neutral port price changed to 50
  2. Cargo limit: 30
  3. When roll two identical dice: move point = point
  4. When in whirl: roll more than 6 to get out

3.5 - Fifth iteration

Amukta (ECE Junior), Zohaib (HCI Master), and I played the game.

Analysis

This playtesting reveals the math problems in this game!!! It's almost impossible to calculate the numbers after drawing one inflation cards (which increases all prices by 20%).

Also, no one want to write on the ship.

Also, the goods availability, and prices, chance too frequently. It's hard for players to make strategic planning.

They also give me the suggestion to use tokens, but I would need a LOT of them, which makes it not so feasible.

Modifications

If people don't want to write the goods on their ship, and I don't want the ship to look dull, why not give them the chance to name their ships!! It must feel good to sail a ship with a name on it. It's a feel of ownership, and it helps to get players immersed.

Then, all trading information can be written on the account book.

To deal with the math problem:

  1. When you get a decimal, convert it to an integer by rounding up (always do this for everything)
  2. I changed the "inflation" cards. Right now, they only change the buy prices at all ports without introducing too much decimals.

3.6 - Sixth iteration

I played the new version with myself. This time I focuses on cards.

Analysis

Cards are still a great idea, but the current design is not perfect. First, improv cards are still troublesome. Second, storm card is too OP. Third, there are too few movement intervention cards (like Frame, Full Sail, Dead Wind). I will make modifications to them

Modifications
  1. For improv cards: make them an optional module that the player can choose whether to include them or not. It would be a nice idea to include them after 2 beers. Also, I changed all of them to involve all players, rather than selecting one player and embarrase him/her.
  2. For the storm card, I introduced dice roll to it. Player will only lose goods if rolls smaller than 7. This makes the game more random and more exciting!
  3. I added more movement intervention cards. Also, one new card: "Tail wind" - get two movement points this turn.
  4. Also a card on combat: "Superior strategy" - instantly win a combat.

4 - Final Rule Set

For descriptions of map elements, cards, and battle mechanics, see the attached sheets in the package.

4.1 - Set up

Place the map on a table. Each player gets to pick a ship, name the ship, and write the name on it. Each player gets an account book, that matches the ship's color. Each player gets 2,000 golds as starting fund.

Then, each player choose a starting port. (The map is symmetric so it doesn't really matter! Choose anyone you want!)

Each player rolls two dices, whoever largest gets to move first.

4.2 - Goal

Whoever gets 20,000 golds first wins!

4.3 - Flow

Player take turns to:

  1. Roll two dices. The movement is the different between two dices. However, if the two dice are identical, like two 5s, the movement point will be 5 instead of 0.
  2. The player can move as he/she wants.
    1. During/before/after movement, player can trade with ports. Player can only trade with ports when sitting inside them.
      1. Trading prices are written on the "price list" sheet that's included in the package. (All additional sheets are also attached to this document)
      2. During/before/after movement, if a player (A) gets adjacent to another player (B)'s ship, or onto another player (B)'s ship, player A can choose to attack player B. See the "Battle mechanics" sheet for more information
        1. Note: each player can only attack once in her/his turn. And attacking doesn't cost movement points.
        2. Then the turn ends. The player draws a card. Move on to the next player's turn.

Notes:

  1. The player can use as many action cards during his/her turn as he/she wants.
  2. If a ship is in the whirld, or in a port, other ships cannot attack it.
  3. For rules on map areas and cards, see attached sheets in the package.

One more thing: Remember to draw a card at the end of your turn.
